# Physical Domain Laboratory

## Overview

The Physical Domain Laboratory provides comprehensive simulation capabilities spanning quantum mechanics to cosmological scales. It enables first-principles modeling of all physical phenomena with configurable resolution and accuracy.

## Core Capabilities

### Quantum Scale
- Quantum field theory calculations
- Particle physics interactions
- Nuclear reactions and decay
- Quantum electrodynamics
- Quantum chromodynamics

### Atomic & Molecular Scale
- Atomic structure and spectra
- Molecular dynamics
- Chemical bonding
- Material properties
- Condensed matter physics

### Classical Scale
- Newtonian mechanics
- Fluid dynamics (CFD)
- Thermodynamics
- Electromagnetism
- Acoustics
- Optics

### Cosmological Scale
- General relativity
- Stellar evolution
- Galactic dynamics
- Cosmological simulations
- Black hole physics

## Resolution Modes

| Mode | Scale Range | Accuracy | Performance |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Quantum | < 1 nm | Highest | Slowest |
| Atomic | 1 nm - 1 μm | High | Moderate |
| Mesoscale | 1 μm - 1 mm | Medium | Fast |
| Continuum | > 1 mm | Macroscopic | Fastest |

## Agent Framework

The laboratory includes specialized agents:
- Quantum mechanics specialists
- Fluid dynamics engineers
- Thermodynamics analysts
- Cosmology researchers
- Materials scientists

## Integration Points

- Connects to Chemical Laboratory for reaction dynamics
- Connects to Materials Laboratory for property prediction
- Connects to Aerospace Laboratory for fluid-structure interaction
- Connects to Quantum Laboratory for quantum hardware simulation
