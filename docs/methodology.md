# Methodology: Sovereign Intelligence Engine

## Introduction
The Sovereign Intelligence Engine (SIE) is designed to model complex systems across multiple domains and scales. Its architecture is structured to maintain rigorous causal integrity while allowing adaptive heuristic discovery and scenario evolution. The Engine emphasizes **structural invariance**, **cross-domain interaction**, and **self-improving intelligence**.

## Architecture Overview

### Node Taxonomy
SIE uses a hierarchical, multi-resolution node taxonomy:
- **R-Nodes (Planetary/Physical):** Represent physical systems, ecosystems, infrastructure, and resources.
- **O-Nodes (Orbital/Interplanetary):** Capture satellite, communication, and interplanetary interactions.
- **Q-Nodes (Quantum/Information):** Model quantum networks, entanglement fidelity, and key distribution systems.
- **D-Nodes (Digital/Virtual):** Represent digital ecosystems, DeFi protocols, cloud regions, and VR/AR networks.
- **Supernodes:** High-centrality nodes across multiple layers; assigned a Supernode Fragility Index (SFI) to track systemic risk.

### Resolution Layers
- **Strategic Layer (Macro):** Annual to quarterly modeling; identifies irreversible thresholds and global cascades.
- **Tactical Layer (Micro):** Monthly to weekly resolution; captures local interactions and short-term dynamics.
- **Adaptive Switching:** Automatically adjusts resolution based on event sensitivity, cross-domain propagation, or heuristic uncertainty.

## Simulation Mechanics

### Constraint-Based Modeling
- Explicitly handles irreversible thresholds and cascading failures.
- Non-linear propagation of events across nodes and domains.
- Stochastic micro-events introduced for robustness and stress-testing.

### Cross-Domain Micro-Events
- Events propagate across biological, social, technical, and infrastructural layers.
- Multi-domain micro-events account for compounding effects, e.g., “Heatwave_Plus_Pandemic_Strain.”
- Micro-events can trigger new latent parameters identified by the Adaptive Parameter Expansion (APE) layer.

### Heuristic Discovery
- **Heuristic Discovery Engine (HDE):** Analyzes residual variance to identify unexplained correlations.
- **Sandbox Integration:** Candidate heuristics are tested before full integration.
- **Self-Improvement:** The system refines its predictive and causal models over time.

### Meta-Federation Orchestration
- External specialized simulators can be invoked for high-complexity domains (e.g., economic crises, quantum events).
- Results are integrated to improve internal heuristics, gradually reducing external dependency.

### Reporting & Reproducibility
- All simulations produce deterministic, serializable scenario objects.
- Automated generation of analytical reports with visualizations, causal chains, and heuristic influence logs.

## Governance
- Exclusive control resides with OmegaCore Research.
- Operational outputs can be disseminated selectively; Engine execution remains internal.
- All modifications, scenario snapshots, and derived insights are logged in `/meta` for reproducibility and auditability.

## Applications
- Strategic foresight and systemic risk analysis
- Complex systems research across multi-domain interactions
- Cross-domain scenario planning and intervention assessment
- Defense, security, and high-stakes decision support
